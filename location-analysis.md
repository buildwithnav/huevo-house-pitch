## 2. The Opportunity

### What Is Huevo House?
A modern breakfast and brunch restaurant that gives the Hispanic community their own Denny's/IHOP — but better. The core menu is straight American breakfast (steak & eggs, pancakes, waffles, omelets, chicken fried steak) with authentic Mexican items woven in (chilaquiles, birria omelets, huevos rancheros, house-made red and green salsas). The interior is quirky and Instagrammable with egg-themed decor and puns throughout.

**The positioning:** Not a Mexican restaurant. Not a generic diner. A modern, fun, high-quality breakfast spot that happens to be made by people who grew up eating real Mexican food — so the salsas are legit, the chilaquiles are right, and the vibe feels like home for Hispanic families while being welcoming and appealing to everyone.

## 7. Strategic Advantages

1. **Proven concept, proven numbers.** Two profitable locations with consistent $24-25/sq ft revenue. This isn't a startup gamble — it's a replication play.

2. **Market gap is real.** Zero modern Hispanic-angle breakfast concepts in Santa Clarita. The competition is a 50-year-old diner and two chain restaurants.

3. **Higher-income market.** Santa Clarita's $140K median income is 82% higher than Bakersfield. Customers here will pay premium prices and tip better.

4. **Turnkey kitchen.** $750K+ buildout already done. Walk-in cooler, freezer, hood system, commercial kitchen line. Your investment goes into cosmetics and branding, not infrastructure.

5. **The patio is a multiplier.** 2,000 sq ft of outdoor seating in Southern California = weekend brunch destination. No Bakersfield location has this asset.

6. **Landlord wants you.** Leverage this for better lease terms, TI allowance, and free rent during buildout.

7. **Owner lives in Santa Clarita.** Hands-on management during the critical launch phase without a 2-hour commute.

8. **Beer & Wine license transfers.** No waiting months for ABC approval. Michelada program, mimosa flights, and brunch cocktails from day one.
